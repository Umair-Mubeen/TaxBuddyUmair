Most Pakistanis lose thousands of rupees every year paying higher withholding tax simply because they haven't filed. This guide explains every benefit, with real numbers and FBR references.

What exactly is a tax filer?

A tax filer is an individual or business who has submitted their annual Income Tax Return for the relevant tax year and whose name appears on the Active Taxpayer List (ATL) published by the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR).

If you have not filed your return, you are categorised as a non-filer — and FBR applies significantly higher withholding tax rates across dozens of everyday transactions, from buying a car to withdrawing cash from your bank.

Core Benefits

7 major benefits of being a filer

Lower withholding tax rates
Filers pay significantly reduced WHT on banking, vehicles, property, dividends, and over 50 other transaction types.
Cheaper property transactions
Buy or sell property at lower advance tax rates. Capital gains treatment also improves for filers under Sections 236C and 236K.
Vehicle registration savings
Non-filers pay nearly double the advance tax on vehicle registration. On a PKR 30 lakh car, the saving can exceed PKR 1.5 lakh.
Lower banking tax
Reduced deductions on cash withdrawals, profit on debt, and other banking transactions. Non-filers face double rates above threshold limits.
Tax refund eligibility
Only filers can claim refunds for excess tax deducted during the year via Section 170. Non-filers lose this money permanently.
Legal protection & compliance
Filer status reduces your exposure to FBR notices and audits. It also strengthens your financial profile for loans and visa applications.

How to become a filer

How to become a tax filer — step by step

The entire process is done online through the FBR's IRIS portal. You don't need to visit any FBR office if your documents are ready.

1
Register on IRIS portal
Visit iris.fbr.gov.pk and register using your CNIC number. You'll receive a password via SMS and email.
2
Obtain your NTN
After IRIS registration, your National Tax Number (NTN) is automatically assigned. For businesses, you may need to register separately.
3
Fill your income tax return
Select the correct return form based on your income type — salaried (IT-1), business individual (IT-2), AOP (IT-3), or company (IT-4).
4
Submit before 30 September
The standard deadline for return filing is 30 September following the close of the tax year (July–June). Filing late attracts penalties under Section 182.
5
Wait for ATL update
Your name appears on the Active Taxpayer List within 24–48 hours of successful submission. ATL is updated every Monday by FBR.

What happens if you don't file?

Non-filing isn't just about paying higher withholding tax rates. Under the Finance Act 2024–25, FBR has introduced additional restrictions for non-compliant taxpayers:

  • Higher withholding tax on all major financial transactions — often double the filer rate
  • Penalty of PKR 40,000 or 0.1% of declared turnover per year for not filing under Section 182
  • Automatic audit selection — non-filers are more likely to receive FBR notices and audit orders
  • No refund eligibility — excess tax deducted throughout the year cannot be reclaimed
  • Restriction on high-value transactions — FBR may restrict property, vehicles, and foreign travel for persistent non-filers
  • Damaged financial profile — banks, visa officers, and lenders check ATL status for loan approvals and travel documentation
🚫
Late filer penalty — Section 182
If you file after 30 September but within 60 days, a penalty of PKR 1,000 per day applies. After 60 days, the penalty increases to PKR 2,000 per day or 0.1% of your income, whichever is higher.
