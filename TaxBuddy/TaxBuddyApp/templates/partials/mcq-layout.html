Beginner Fundamentals of Pakistan Tax 5 questions
1
What does ATL stand for in the context of FBR Pakistan?
ITO 2001
A
Annual Tax List
B
Active Taxpayer List
C
Authorized Taxpayer Ledger
D
Adjusted Tax Liability
Active Taxpayer List (ATL) is published by FBR and contains names of individuals who have filed their income tax return. Filers on ATL enjoy lower withholding tax rates. Reference: Section 181A, ITO 2001.
2
What is the standard income tax return filing deadline for individuals in Pakistan?
ITO 2001
A
30 June
B
31 December
C
30 September
D
31 March
The standard deadline for filing an income tax return is 30 September following the end of the tax year (July–June). Reference: Section 118, ITO 2001.
3
What is NTN in the context of Pakistan taxation?
ITO 2001
A
National Transaction Number
B
National Tax Number
C
Net Tax Notation
D
Nominal Tax Numeral
NTN (National Tax Number) is a unique identifier assigned by FBR to every registered taxpayer — individuals, companies, and AOPs. Required for filing returns and conducting most financial transactions. Reference: Section 181, ITO 2001.
4
Pakistan's tax year runs from:
ITO 2001
A
January 1 to December 31
B
July 1 to June 30
C
April 1 to March 31
D
October 1 to September 30
Pakistan's normal tax year runs from 1st July to 30th June of the following year. A special tax year may be approved by the Commissioner if a different period is required. Reference: Section 74, ITO 2001.
5
Which government body administers income tax in Pakistan?
General
A
State Bank of Pakistan
B
Securities & Exchange Commission
C
Federal Board of Revenue (FBR)
D
Ministry of Finance
Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) is the apex tax authority in Pakistan responsible for administering and collecting income tax, sales tax, customs duties, and federal excise duty.

Intermediate Withholding Tax & Deductions 5 questions
6
Which section of ITO 2001 deals with payments to non-residents?
WHT
A
Section 147
B
Section 148
C
Section 151
D
Section 152
Section 152 deals with deduction of tax at source from payments of royalty, fees for technical services, and other payments made to non-resident persons. Reference: Section 152, ITO 2001.
7
Under Section 236C, advance tax is collected on the:
Property
A
Purchase of immovable property
B
Sale or transfer of immovable property
C
Rental income from property
D
Construction of property
Section 236C requires collection of advance tax from the seller at the time of sale or transfer of immovable property. Section 236K applies to the purchaser. Reference: Section 236C, ITO 2001.
8
Section 153 of ITO 2001 deals with payments for:
WHT
A
Salary and wages
B
Dividend income
C
Goods, services, and contracts
D
Prize and winnings
Section 153 covers withholding tax deductions on payments for goods, services, and execution of contracts. It is one of the most widely applied WHT provisions affecting businesses. Reference: Section 153, ITO 2001.
9
What is the income tax rate for a Small Company in Tax Year 2026?
Tax Rates
A
20%
B
25%
C
29%
D
21%
A Small Company (registered on or after 1 July 2005 under Companies Act 2017) is taxed at 21% for Tax Year 2026. Reference: First Schedule, Part I, Division II, ITO 2001.
10
Section 7E of ITO 2001 relates to tax on:
Property
A
Foreign remittances
B
Prize winnings
C
Deemed income from immovable property
D
Profit on bank deposits
Section 7E taxes deemed income from capital value of immovable properties at 1% of fair market value. Certain properties are exempt, including one self-occupied residential property. Reference: Section 7E, ITO 2001.

Advanced Complex Provisions & Sales Tax 5 questions
11
Under which section is a person liable to pay advance tax on a quarterly basis?
Advance Tax
A
Section 136
B
Section 140
C
Section 147
D
Section 150
Section 147 requires certain taxpayers (companies, AOPs, and individuals with prior year tax liability exceeding PKR 1 million) to pay advance tax in quarterly instalments. Reference: Section 147, ITO 2001.
12
Section 170 of ITO 2001 deals with:
Refunds
A
Penalty for late filing
B
Assessment of income
C
Refund of excess tax paid
D
Transfer pricing adjustments
Section 170 provides the mechanism for a taxpayer to claim a refund for excess tax paid or deducted. A refund application must be filed within two years of the later of the assessment date or the excess payment date. Reference: Section 170, ITO 2001.
13
Under the Sales Tax Act 1990, who is a "Tier-1 Retailer"?
STA 1990
A
Any retailer with annual sales above PKR 5 million
B
A retailer listed on the stock exchange
C
A retailer operating from a air-conditioned shop in a plaza, mall, or commercial area as defined
D
Any retailer who imports goods directly
A Tier-1 Retailer includes retailers operating from air-conditioned shops in plazas, malls, or commercial areas with certain floor area thresholds, or those with annual turnover exceeding specified limits. Reference: Section 2(43A), STA 1990.
14
Section 182 of ITO 2001 deals with:
Penalties
A
Audit of taxpayer accounts
B
Penalty for failure to furnish a return or statement
C
Recovery of tax from third parties
D
Appeals to Commissioner Inland Revenue
Section 182 prescribes penalties for failure to furnish a return, statement, or other document. Penalty of PKR 1,000 per day for first 60 days, then PKR 2,000 per day or 0.1% of taxable income — whichever is higher. Reference: Section 182, ITO 2001.
15
Under Section 7B of ITO 2001, tax on profit on debt exceeding Rs. 5 million is treated as:
Special Tax
A
Exempt from tax
B
Business income taxable at normal rates
C
Taxable at 15% as a separate block of income
D
Adjustable against total income
Where profit on debt exceeds PKR 5 million, Section 7B treats the entire amount as a separate block of income taxable at 15%. Below PKR 5 million, it is taxed at 15% under Section 151 as a final tax. Reference: Section 7B, ITO 2001.
