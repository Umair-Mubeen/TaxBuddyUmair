Key terms to know
Resident vs Non-Resident
A person is resident if present in Pakistan for ≥183 days in a tax year. Residents are taxed on worldwide income.
Tax Year
Pakistan's normal tax year runs from 1 July to 30 June (e.g. Tax Year 2026 = July 2025 – June 2026).
Taxable Income
Total income minus deductions and allowances. The amount on which tax is actually calculated.
AOP
Association of Persons — a partnership or joint venture. Taxed as a separate entity under ITO 2001.

Chapter 4
Return Filing on IRIS
How to file your income tax return on the FBR IRIS portal — step by step.
1
Register on IRIS portal
Visit iris.fbr.gov.pk → Register → Enter your CNIC. You will receive your login credentials via SMS and email. Your NTN is automatically assigned.
2
Select the correct return form
Choose your form based on income type: IT-1 (salaried), IT-2 (business individual), IT-3 (AOP), IT-4 (company). Most individuals use IT-1 or IT-2.
3
Enter income and deduction details
Fill salary/income details, withholding tax deducted, adjustable taxes, tax credits (Section 62 for investment, Section 64 for donations), and assets/liabilities (wealth statement).
4
Compute and verify tax liability
IRIS will auto-calculate tax. Verify against your own calculation using the slab rates. If excess tax was deducted, you may be eligible for a refund under Section 170.
5
Submit before 30 September
Standard deadline is 30 September following the close of the tax year. Late filing attracts penalties of PKR 1,000/day for first 60 days, then PKR 2,000/day under Section 182.
⚠️
Wealth Statement is mandatory
All return filers must also submit a wealth statement (Statement of Assets and Liabilities). Failure to file a wealth statement makes the return incomplete and may trigger notices.
