Key terms in Sales Tax Act 1990
Taxable Supply
A supply of taxable goods made in Pakistan by a registered person in the course of taxable activity. Section 2(41).
Tax Period
A calendar month. Sales tax returns must be filed by the 18th of the following month.
Output Tax
Sales tax charged on supplies made. Collected from buyers and payable to FBR.
Input Tax
Sales tax paid on purchases. Deductible against output tax — you only pay tax on the net difference.
Registered Person
A person registered under the Sales Tax Act 1990 and holding a valid registration certificate.
Tax Invoice
A document issued by a registered person showing the amount of tax charged. Required for input tax claims.

Zero-rated vs exempt — key difference
Zero-rated goods are still taxable supplies — the supplier can claim input tax refunds. Exempt goods are outside the tax net entirely — no input tax can be claimed on expenses related to exempt supplies.

How to register for sales tax on IRIS
1
Login to IRIS portal
Visit iris.fbr.gov.pk and login with your existing NTN credentials. If you don't have an NTN, register for income tax first to obtain one.
2
Navigate to Registration
Go to Registration → Sales Tax Registration → New Application. Select the appropriate category (manufacturer, importer, exporter, retailer, service provider).
3
Fill business details
Enter business name, type, address, bank account details, description of taxable activity, and estimated monthly turnover. Upload required documents (CNIC, utility bills, lease/ownership proof).
4
Submit and await verification
Submit the application. FBR may conduct a physical verification visit. Upon approval, your Sales Tax Registration Number (STRN) is issued within 2–5 working days.
5
Display registration certificate
Every registered person must display their registration certificate prominently at their business premises and issue proper tax invoices from the date of registration.
⚠️
Voluntary registration is also permitted
Even if you are below the mandatory registration threshold, you can register voluntarily. This allows you to charge sales tax, issue tax invoices, and claim input tax adjustment — beneficial if your buyers are registered persons.
Chapter 4
Tier-1 Retailers
Who qualifies as a Tier-1 retailer, their obligations, and the fixed tax scheme for Tier-2.
Tier-1 Retailer
Registered & fully taxable
Must register for sales tax, charge 18% GST, issue tax invoices, file monthly returns, and maintain records.
  • Air-conditioned shop in plaza/mall
  • Annual turnover above specified threshold
  • Chain stores / franchise retailers
  • Importers selling directly to consumers
  • Distributors operating as retailers
Tier-2 Retailer
Fixed tax via electricity bill
Small retailers not falling in Tier-1. Pay a fixed sales tax added to their electricity bill — no monthly return filing required.
  • Small shops not in malls/plazas
  • Below annual turnover threshold
  • Non-air-conditioned outlets
  • Kirana stores and local traders
  • No tax invoice requirement

Net tax formula
Net Tax Payable = Output Tax − Input Tax. If input tax exceeds output tax in a given month, the excess is carried forward to the next tax period or claimed as a refund (for exporters).
Chapter 6
Sales Tax Return Filing
How and when to file monthly sales tax returns on IRIS.
1
Maintain sales and purchase registers
Record all taxable supplies made (sales/output) and all taxable purchases (input). Each entry must be supported by a valid tax invoice. Keep records for 5 years.
2
Login to IRIS and open Sales Tax Return
Go to IRIS portal → Returns → Sales Tax Return. Select the relevant tax period (month). The return form auto-populates from your sales data if you've been issuing e-invoices.
3
Enter output and input tax
Fill in total taxable sales and output tax charged. Then fill taxable purchases and input tax available. Declare any blocked input tax (personal use, passenger vehicles, etc.).
4
Calculate and pay net tax
Net tax = Output Tax − Input Tax. If positive, pay via PSID/CPR before filing. If negative (excess input), carry forward or apply for refund (exporters only).
5
Submit by 18th of following month
The deadline for monthly sales tax returns is the 18th of the month following the tax period. Late filing attracts a penalty of PKR 10,000 per return under Section 33.

🚫
Fake invoices — zero tolerance
FBR has a dedicated unit tracking fake or flying invoice fraud. Using fake tax invoices to claim false input tax credits can result in arrest, prosecution, and imprisonment under Section 37A of STA 1990.
